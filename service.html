Urine Test

A urine test checks for various conditions, including infections and kidney disease.

Blood Test

A blood test measures various substances in the blood and can help diagnose diseases.

X-ray

An X-ray is used to view the internal structures of the body, especially bones.

MRI Scan

An MRI creates detailed images of organs and tissues within the body.

Ultrasound

An ultrasound uses sound waves to produce images of organs inside the body.

Urine Test

Causes:

Infections, kidney disease, diabetes.

Recommendations:

Stay hydrated, consult a healthcare professional.

Home Remedies:

Drink plenty of water, cranberry juice may help.

What to Do:

Follow your doctor’s instructions for the test.

Depends on the underlying cause; follow medical advice.

Blood Test

Causes:

Various health conditions, deficiencies.

Recommendations:

Regular health check-ups, maintain a balanced diet.

Home Remedies:

Include iron-rich foods, stay hydrated.

What to Do:

Discuss results with your healthcare provider.

Treatment:

Based on specific deficiencies or conditions identified.

X-ray

Causes:

Fractures, infections, tumors.

Recommendations:

Consult a doctor for interpretation of results.

Home Remedies:

Rest, ice application for minor injuries.

What to Do:

Follow up with your healthcare provider.

Treatment:

Depends on the diagnosis; may include medications or physical therapy.

MRI Scan

Causes:

Detecting tumors, brain disorders, joint issues.

Recommendations:

Inform your doctor of any metal implants.

Home Remedies:

Stay calm, practice deep breathing during the procedure.

What to Do:

Follow pre-scan instructions carefully.

Treatment:

Based on the results; may involve surgery, medication, or therapy.

Ultrasound

Causes:

Pregnancy monitoring, diagnosing organ issues.

Recommendations:

Discuss results with your doctor.

Home Remedies:

Stay hydrated, eat light before the test.

What to Do:

Follow your doctor’s advice for follow-up.

Treatment:

Based on findings; may involve further testing or treatment.
